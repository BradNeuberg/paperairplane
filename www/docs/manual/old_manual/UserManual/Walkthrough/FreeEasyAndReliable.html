Walkthrough - Seamless Collaboration

Free, Easy, and Reliable

Creating advanced online communities has never been easier. Simply start up the Paper Airplane browser and use the included wizards to create a Two Way Web Site. It's as simple as that.

Two Way Web Sites are also completely free to create and administer. While Paper Airplane is free in terms of money, every participant must still “pay” some of their computer's space and bandwidth to store pieces of the Two Way Web. The net result of this is that the web is transformed into a giant, free hard-drive, where creating sites and pages is as easy as clicking your mouse. As users sign in and out of the peer-to-peer Two Way Web, site pieces are moved between peers to maintain reliability. Since Paper Airplane also banishes centralized servers, hordes of system administrators are also not needed to maintain your web site. You can turn off your machine without the fear that your web site will disappear.

Some companies spend millions of dollars to create replicated server farms, setting up portions of their web sites in far-flung locations such as Tokyo and California to ensure that if disaster befalls an area the web site will keep on ticking. You get this kind of reliability for free with Paper Airplane. Two Way Web Sites are broken into hundreds of pieces and spread across the earth on the hard-drives of other people running the Paper Airplane browser. Each piece is encrypted to prevent tampering on the personal computer it is stored on, and multiple copies of the piece are made to ensure that if one user signs off of Paper Airplane that portion will still be available. When users request your Two Way Web Site, pieces will began re-assembling themselves from all over the globe. If a portion has been tampered with or changed, the Paper Airplane browser will detect this and invalidate that piece. Even if some storage personal computers are knocked out or some are overwhelmed, your web site will keep on trucking. Now you can have the reliability of the Fortune 500 without spending millions of dollars.
