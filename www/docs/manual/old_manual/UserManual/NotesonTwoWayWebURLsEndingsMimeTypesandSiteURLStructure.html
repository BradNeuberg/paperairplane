Notes on Two Way Web URLs, Endings, Mime-Types, and Site URL Structure

[Put exact site structure URLs and URL format here after being resolved in the Two Way Web Architecture document]


The exact format and mechanisms of Two Way Web URLs will be decided based on architectural issues, but the following is known now. Just as the web has the ubiquitous http:// protocol-handler before every web-site name, Paper Airplane has go://. The go:// portion is a clue to the Paper Airplane browser to use the Two Way Web peer-to-peer protocol. Following go:// is the DisDNS domain name, such as www.CodingInParadise.weblog, followed by the actual page to retrieve. For example, go://www.CodingInParadise.weblog/DoingBusinessInBangkok.page will load the DoingBusinessInBangkok page into the browser.

The go:// protocol simply retrieves a page for viewing; the edit:// protocol will initiate editing a document using the Two Way Web peer-to-peer protocol. For example, edit://www.CodingInParadise.weblog/DoingBusinessInBangkok.page will start editing the DoingBusinessInBangkok page.

The file-name ending for all Two Way Web pages is .page

Two Way Web Pages can have spaces and can be composed of letters and numbers. They can not have punctuation symbols due to security issues, such as:

? (question mark)

" (double-apostrophe)

' (single-apostrophe)

. (period)

The only punctuation symbol allowed is the comma and the dash to promote conversational page names.
