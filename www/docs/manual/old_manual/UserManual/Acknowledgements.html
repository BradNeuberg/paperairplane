Acknowledgements

 

Software Acknowledgements

Paper Airplane is inspired by and depends on the software innovations of many other people. Thank you very much to:

  • Ward Cunningham for WikiWikiWeb. WikiWiki is the most important ancestor of Paper Airplane. Ward showcased a huge number of innovations: the power of making every page editable, using AutoMagic and WikiNames for organic site growth, the crazy yet profound idea of having web sites that are completely open for users to grow and change, and the simple concept of bundling the tools necessary to change a site with the site itself. Thank you Ward for transforming what the web is about.
  • Mozilla.org and Netscape/AOL for Mozilla. Without Mozilla Paper Airplane would not be possible. Paper Airplane directly uses the Mozilla source code, including its profound technical innovations such as XUL, to make this a feasible project. Thank you for producing such a fast, full-featured, standards-compliant web browser.
  • gobe for gobeProductive. Paper Airplane is directly powered by the full-featured gobeProductive office suite. Thank you for such an amazing suite of tools that redefines what office packages should be.
  • JXTA.org, Bill Joy, and Mike Clary for JXTA. Without JXTA, Paper Airplane would not be possible. JXTA solves a many of the hard-problems of creating a peer to peer system, such as building an elegant, virtual networking layer above firewalled and NATed peers, and more. Thank you for such a well-documented and powerful system.
